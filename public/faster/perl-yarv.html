Back in April 2010, Russ Cox charitably suggested there might be "fair" comparisons for: fannkuch-redux, fasta, k-nucleotide, mandlebrot, nbody, reverse-complement and spectral-norm. As someone who implements programming languages, his interest is "measuring the quality of the generated code when both compilers are presented with what amounts to the same program."
